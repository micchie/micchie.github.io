Michio Honda

Lecturer (Assistant Professor) at the University of Edinburgh first.last@ed.ac.uk

I am a computer scientist in networking and operating systems. I have been a Lecturer (Assistant Professor in US terms) in Networked Systems at the School of Informatics of the University of Edinburgh since 2020. Earlier in my career, I was at NEC Labs Europe (2012-2014 & 2016-2019) and NetApp (2014-2016).

My best known work is perhaps identifying TCP extensibility against middlebox interference (IMC'11) and building the first TCP/IP network stack that abstracts persistent memory (NSDI'18).
Current projects include a software stack for accelerator devices (HotStorage'20), content-aware routing for encrypted traffic (NSDI'21) and networking/storage co-design (HotNets'21).
